The ideal entrance mats

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

The doorway of a home functions as a literal and symbolic welcome. It invites the world inside. The outside meets the inside. Luckily, an entrance mat keeps your home clean, inviting, and safe. To help you choose the right one for your property, we have a little guide. We are going to break the potential options down to help you make an informed choice.

Types

Recessed mats provide a clean and sleek look. They do so whilst effectively trapping moisture and dirt that's flush with the floor. Roll-out mats supply continuous coverage. You can also cut them to the necessary length. That is preferable for long entrance areas. Scraper mats are made to remove debris and dirt effectively from shoes. They are best for situations where people may have been walking through mud. Modular tiles work well for areas with distinct layouts. Not to mention, they are versatile and simple to replace. You will also want to consider different entrance mats for every season. When the seasons change, you will need something that can adapt to the weather conditions.

Spring

During spring, entryways become wet and muddy as the season brings melting snow and rain. Concentrate on dirt removal and moisture absorption. To effectively trap mud and moisture, go for absorbent materials like coir and microfibre.

Summer

In summer, there is an increase in dust and dry dirt making its way indoors. This is from a combination of exterior activities and dry weather. You should use materials like nylon to effectively capture and retain dry dirt particles.

Autumn

Next is a season characterised by unpredictable rain showers and falling leaves. Both need to be managed by your entrance mat. Select mats for autumn that permit water to drain through with open patterns that are also able to trap leaves.

Winter

Finally, with winter, it introduces the exclusive challenges of ice, snow, and the use of de-icing salt. Heavy-duty rubber mats can withstand the harshness. To stop people slipping on icy days, ensure your mat has wonderful traction.
