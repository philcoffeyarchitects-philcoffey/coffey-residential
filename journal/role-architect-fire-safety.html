The role of an architect in fire safety

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

We are an establishment capable of taking on any project without ever losing sight of the primary objective. Formed back in 2005, we have since developed an outstanding reputation for excellence in the field. As such, we are the ones everyone prefers to contact when things need doing quickly and efficiently. As architects, whilst creating buildings, we must review both the health of the public and the fire protection present in the premises. We need to look at the property from each angle, including that of inhabitants and first responders. The architect's role with safety is imperative, and we are in fact the first individuals who must think about it. All designs must comply with the property rules that apply in our country. Additionally, we have to think proactively. It's true that building codes are the guidelines for safety. However, they typically come with major concerns to address, including layout issues. What kinds of layout issues need addressing? The flow and layout of a structure can determine how rapidly fire fighters can do their job. There should be easy access and dedicated routes to use. Some focus on staircase design, there should be distinct emphasis on landing width, numbers, and length. Architects can also look into whether elevators or stairs would work best during fire rescue scenarios. At Coffey Architects, we create residential architecture residents don't just find aesthetically pleasing; we also add value for their owners. Our designs have attracted the attention of various well-known bodies, and have gained numerous rewards as a result. You can have a look at some of them on our website. We hope they will inspire you. If you are interested in working with us, please give our team a call.
