Consider shower walk-ins and forgo chrome hardware

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

When building in London, it can be a challenge to create exactly what you want. Secondly, the distinct architectural setting can complicate matters. There are rules to limit what you can do in certain areas. With our team helping you though, you will be able to succeed.

Do you have some good ideas for your shower? If so, there is a lot to consider with these small, but hard working areas. By having a shower room, you can easily save space in a compact bathroom. Removing your bath and constructing a huge shower enclosure shall give you sufficient room to move around. It can also help with accessibility. First things first, you need to decide what you want. In this post, we will be looking at some of the areas that you should concentrate on for your shower room.

A walk-in

With one of these showers, you will be able to maximise the sense of space in any room. Something people have asked before is if they should get rid of their bath in favour of a bigger shower space. It does come down to personal preference. You will be able to integrate a bath wonderfully into a wet room. However, there is just something that is special about a huge shower area.

Forget the chrome

Another detail you should think about is forgoing classic chrome hardware. It is true that chrome radiators, showers, and taps were once en vogue. However, one of the bigger trends with bathrooms at the moment is picking black hardware instead. There is no need to stop there either. Go for black bathroom accessories, mirrors, skins, and furniture, and bring the entire room together. It can make the space feel warmer in the process and gives a look.
