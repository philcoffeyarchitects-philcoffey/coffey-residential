Safe and colourful playroom suggestions

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Being architects, we dedicate our lives and talents towards creating something very special for the clients. Regardless of what their project entails, whether they are after a slight alteration or an entirely new complex altogether, we see to it that all of their specifications are met to the highest of standards. If you are looking to design a playroom for your kids, you can go about this in many ways. Best of all, it can be accomplished without breaking the bank. After-all, children typically don't require much to keep themselves entertained. We have some ideas of our own on this subject, and we'd be more than willing to share them with you if you're currently lacking inspiration. With decorating, safety comes first every time, regardless of your child's age. Even teenagers need to be kept safe in their own spaces. For younger children, do some research on safety concerns such as lead paint, corded blinds, and cabinet doors, and factor the findings into your blueprint. Also, go for soft storage bins and boxes; heavier varieties can be pulled from the shelf with ease, leading to injury. Next, we move onto colour. Selecting a theme or palette for the room can be a rather fun experience in truth. In times like these, it's a wonderful opportunity to go bold. Youngsters do tend to respond to big patterns and bright colours, but don't be hesitant to veer away from the primary shades. All sorts of modern colours exist that would work well in a playroom, yet still make it seem grown up enough that the parents don't feel as if they're in a kid exclusive area. At Coffey Architects, whenever we're given the chance, we create and unique pieces of architecture that really stand out from the crowd. Throughout the years, our projects have been recognised and rewarded by official bodies like RIBA for being some of the best ever produced, and we hope to continue making fantastic articles in the future.
