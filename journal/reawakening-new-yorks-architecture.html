The reawakening of New York's architecture

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

Architects in London and throughout the world have long looked to New York for inspiration, as it is the home of some of the world's most iconic buildings. Now there's an exciting new addition to the city skyline. It took thirteen years to erect, but One World Trade Center has finally opened doors to its first tenants. Publishing giant Conde Nast is set to be the first resident of the brand new building. The official opening date was the 3rd of November 2014 and the tower is built on the site of the old Twin Towers, adjacent to the 9/11 Memorial and Museum. Its 140 storeys extend 540 metres into the sky, and it has ceremoniously been named the tallest building in the western hemisphere. With a steel-reinforced concrete structure, it is designed to be as durable as possible. Eventually, the building's observation deck will be open to the public, offering unbeatable views for miles in every direction. The brand new tower is helping the area to continue to thrive. Besides the many financial firms which have always made their home in this part of Lower Manhattan, there are many successful businesses, shops, and restaurants in the local area, as well as thousands more residents who have moved into the area in the past few years. As architects in London, we are committed to providing the finest architectural services for both commercial and residential projects of all types.
