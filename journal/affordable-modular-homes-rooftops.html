Affordable modular homes for rooftops

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

There is an amazing amount of choice, whether you want a flat in a high rise or a period family home. There are also a lot of special homes that are unique to suit challenging sites or requirements.

Modular homes on rooftops

People in London will start to see more and more rooftop developments in the next decade. This is because more and more councils are starting to back these projects. Adding more storeys on to existing buildings is a great way to create homes and does not require any additional land. There have already been a number of projects involving rooftop extensions and installing new storeys. However in the next decade there are plans to create 10,000 more units. This could create a lot more affordable housing across the capital. The lack of land cost means that the price for the homes can be much lower, therefore giving people more chance to buy a home.

Why modular?

There are many reasons why modular homes are growing in popularity, whether they are for rooftop extensions or traditional developments. Most importantly they are quicker and cheaper to build. Most of the structure can be prefabricated and delivered to site ready to fit together and finish. This is great for reducing disruption and the labour cost of projects. Modular homes can be very eco friendly and sustainable. They tend to be made from renewable resources, including timber composites. The lower weight also means smaller foundations, consuming fewer resources.
