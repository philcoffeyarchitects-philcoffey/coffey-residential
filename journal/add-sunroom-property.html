Add a sunroom to your property

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Have you ever considered making some home improvements? There are lots of things you can do to add value and make homes better for the inhabitants. If you are itching to add something, a sunroom could be a great option. You can design the perfect space with our help. After all, Coffey Architects is one of the top names in residential architecture.

Sunrooms can make a fine addition to almost any property, with everyone being able to enjoy them. People often confuse them with conservatories, but there are some important differences. They can be usable for more of the year and tend to be made almost entirely of glass. You can also typically build a sunroom much quicker; generally it takes just a day or two, or a handful if it is a full garden room. The right sunroom design can offer a lot of benefits. To give you an idea of what you'd be in for, we are going to discuss some of the best things about them here.

Energy savings

To start with, you could potentially lower your energy bill. Paying the utility bills isn't the nicest thing to have on your mind. However, we can't really avoid them. We need light, which means using electricity most days. Well, this isn't always the case. With a sunroom, you are basically giving yourself a whole space that will be flooded with sunlight for most of the day. Even when it is cloudy, the room can have enough brightness for you to see clearly. By using the sunroom for activities, you can lower your need for artificial lighting (and electricity) considerably.

Basking in the light

Another advantage is you being able to bask in the natural sunlight. It can be annoying moving your chair repeatedly to where the sun is, especially when you are trying to read. Not only can you relax in the gentle natural light, but you can experience the warmth. A sunroom is the perfect place to spend time in the morning with a coffee or book. Natural sunlight has its own benefits as well, improving our happiness and health.

Additional space

Finally, you can use it for extra space. If you feel like everything is getting a bit crowded, adding to the house will help you spread things out. Any additional room can meet this need. But, with a sunroom there is another advantage of it functioning as an individual getaway space. Sunrooms can feel and look more like an individual sanctuary. It could be your private space if you want, and you can even have greenery too.
