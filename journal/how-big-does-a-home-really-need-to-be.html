How big does a home really need to be?

Words · Simon Welch · 2 min read

Some people love living in small, compact homes where they have everything they need and minimal clutter. Others want larger properties with all kinds of amenities. Generally it comes down to preferences and lifestyle. However, you need to be careful to ensure you won't outgrow the space or suddenly find you're only occupying a small piece of a giant property.

Thinking about the future

One of the main reasons people choose big homes is they are planning ahead. They want a home for life so there is no need to move. As a result, they ensure they've got space if there are any changes in the future, such as having children, elderly parents moving in, and home workspace.

This kind of thinking can provide some major benefits. Firstly, it ensures you avoid the disruption of needing to find a larger house, pack up and move. More importantly, it can mean avoiding having to pay stamp duty when you move up the housing ladder. It can save thousands of pounds.

Is it still a measure of value?

For a long time larger homes were also a way to display wealth. Having dozens of bedrooms and bathrooms, as well as all the extras like a home cinema, pool, wine cellar, and gym, made a home very valuable. That still applies today to some extent, but there has been a change in attitudes. Now, more people are displaying wealth by focusing on sustainability and cutting back on excess.

Even more interestingly, there is a focus on nature today. The idea of "landscape as a" is really taking off.

Is open plan a problem?

An interesting factor to look at is open plan living. It is the preference today, especially for the main living spaces. However, it could actually lead to people creating bigger properties than they need. They may create extra rooms for privacy to offset the fact the main kitchen, dining area and lounge are connected. Would it be better to think of ways you can subdivide open plan areas when you need private spaces?

The storage conundrum

A major thing that can lead to people needing bigger homes is they require more storage. Whether it is a double garage for vehicles or rooms specifically for collections of vinyl or books, some owners dedicate a huge amount of space to storing their possessions. Could downsizing be an option?

What do you need?

The most important thing is to focus on what you need. Choosing a home that is way too large is a bad idea. It can result in higher daily costs and a bigger impact on the environment than is necessary. At the other end of the spectrum, you don't want to be living in a home that is too small and having a negative impact on your wellbeing.
