Does zinc have a bright future in architecture?

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Architecture is the name of the game with our company and throughout the years, we have done everything we can to deliver beautiful results to our clients. From house extensions and refurbishments to libraries and business headquarters, there's nothing that is off limits to us. Green structures are those buildings that are intentionally created to utilise natural resources in environmentally friendly manners. These days, as opposed to being a, they have become something of a necessity. Of the multiple ways to lower lighting, heating and cooling loads, it's recommended that one use high-performance building envelopes. These are roofs, walls and a host of other assemblies that are based around long-term insulation, as well as durability specifications. Right now, zinc stands as one of the best materials for high-performance and sustainability in the world. It has been reported that it is the 23rd most abundant element that's present in the planet's crust. Additionally, it's a non-ferrous substance that can withstand both corrosion and rust, not to mention being immune to the detrimental effects that come from UV rays. This ensures that it possesses an extensive service life without the threat of degradation. All of this is made possible due to the fact that architectural zinc advances its own layer of protection, known as zinc hydroxyl-carbonate. After formation, this layer obstructs chemicals and moisture, preventing them from penetrating the material. Furthermore, the hydroxyl-carbonate shall reform as time passes should it become scratched. At Coffey Architects, in order to bring our client's vision to life, we make it a point to work closely alongside them throughout the process. As time has progressed, we have earned a reputation for creating intelligent and remarkably crafted structures, which add value for our customers in a creative way.
