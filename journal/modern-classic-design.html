The modern classic design

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

To stand out with building designs, you must think of ideas that others would not consider. This is something we have been excelling at for years. The team here at Coffey Architects has an uncanny ability to think outside the box. We deliver something special to each client. One style that might appear on your radar is the modern classic design. This came from European culture originally. Similar to Art Deco and Victorian, the Roman and Greek styles are also primary inspirations for it. Mixing elements of these, the outcome is the European modern classic internal design.

Furnishings

This style's furniture is a mixture of the classic and modern styles. There is also a hint of minimalism. That explains why pieces often have a simple design with few complex pieces of decor. The furniture here is not exactly contemporary. However, it is still very popular today. It is straightforward and does not have as much carving on it as older decorative furnishings. The primary colours for this style are white, silver, grey, grey blue shades, and brown.

Making things more attractive

You can also make this style more attractive. To do so, you need to adorn the space with a number of nice accessories. Just don't exaggerate it. You can pick decorations like a single flower or a vase. Other options include geometric patterned throw pillows and putting a big painting on the wall. With the windows, you could leave them bare to bring in extra sunshine. If you don't want that much light though, you can use blinds for straightforward window treatments. Again you don't want to go too decorative and busy.

Flooring

With the flooring, it is typically neutral with a traditional design. Wood floors in warm tones and soft colour carpets are most common. Since the floor covering is neutral, the texture of the rugs or carpets is vital. Good choices for transitional styles include sisal rugs, hides, and Berber carpet.
