Designing a small balcony

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

It is possible to construct new buildings in the most unexpected places. They can be a success as well if you choose the right design. For this to happen though, you need to work with a team that understands what it is doing. Ours is a business that's worked in architecture for more than a decade, winning awards along the way. You must think of the mood you wish to set when you step outside. Considerations such as these will aid you in selecting decor that is functional and visually appealing. This is vital for you being able to relax and enjoy your space.

Your choice of decor

When you have a plan in place, the fun can begin. You can decorate your small balcony to suit your personal style. Fill it with greenery and plants for a rejuvenating beginning to your mornings. Or, you can concentrate on quality seating if you are going to create an external living room for family and friends. An inherent separation exists between the balcony and the rest of your property. You are able to play this up or minimise it to match your preferences. Sometimes it is great to have a connection between the spaces. However, in other cases you want them to feel separate. Let's say your home decor is more on the classical side. Here, you can attempt to organise floor pillows around a low coffee table on the balcony for a Bohemian-inspired, unexpected alcove. This can work surprisingly well for residential architecture, including balconies on tall blocks. You might be living in a warmer climate and can keep the doors open during the day. If so, you can create a more seamless transition between your external and internal spaces. You can do so by linking your balcony design to the adjacent room. Whatever your choice, the furniture needs weatherproofing.

Restrictions

Don't allow a small space to restrict your balcony design either. It is possible to add much character to one with the correct choice of accessories and furniture. If you're lacking floor space, amplify the cosy feeling by hanging string lights from the railing. Also, take a minimalist approach to avoid overcrowding. When there isn't much room, concentrate on eye-catching details or quality design. This will make your small balcony appear intentional and curated. Versatile solutions such as expandable tables or folding chairs are excellent here. You can pull them out in the right situations, without them being in the way when they are unnecessary.
