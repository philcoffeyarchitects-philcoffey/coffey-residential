Always consider three important F's

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

A number of different trends, rules, and other factors influence every aspect of design. This is true with architecture, interiors, fashion, and much more. Some you can ignore or only apply for a short amount of time. Others are timeless. This is especially true of three crucial F's. We want to look at them today with you.

What are they?

The three terms here are floorplan, finishes, and fixtures. The trio are all vital and work together to ensure you create the perfect spaces. Starting with the floorplan, this is the foundation of everything. It details the layout of the space, letting you map out where things will go. Generally there can be different ways to organise individual spaces. So, you need to find the most effective one. Ensure it will satisfy your needs and make the best use of the space. Secondly, you have the finishes. Once you decide on the floorplan, you can determine the basis of the design by choosing how to finish the walls, floor, and ceiling. These are among the most costly elements and are also some of the hardest to change. Therefore, you need to make the right choices. Finally, you have the fixtures. These can also be rather costly, but typically you have more freedom to change them. For example, if you decide you don't like a particular light fitting, it is relatively easy to swap it out for a new one. You can have the most fun here to ensure the spaces suit your lifestyle and reflect your personality.

Combinations

If you want to create the most impressive residential architecture, you need to utilise the 3 F's. You can do this more easily if you are designing a home from the ground up. Here you can consider the site and then plan the ideal floorplan. You will have more freedom to decide the shape of different parts of the home to accommodate everything you want. A really good idea is to look at how you can combine the finishes and fittings to get the best results. Doing it effectively can create richer, more enjoyable spaces. What you need to look at is things like the textures, colours, and patterns. The best thing to do is take a holistic approach. Think of how every element will impact the whole property. Even simple things like the location of doors and windows can have a massive impact. Would they allow a better layout if you have them elsewhere?
