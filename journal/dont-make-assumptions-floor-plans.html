Don't make assumptions with your floor plans

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

We are a team of architects that goes above and beyond to assist our clients. Our people can design buildings with certain qualities. For example, they can be sustainable and green. Moreover, they can add value for the owner. Most people pick a home they believe is perfect for them and the family. After they move in though, some wonder why the floor plan does not work for them. When trying to find a suitable layout, it is easy to make mistakes. The following are some of the most common ones; read on to stand a better chance of avoiding them.

Presumption

Firstly, don't presume that each plan fits your lifestyle. Start by evaluating how you live prior to selecting a floor plan. You might prefer a multi-storey house or a single-storey one. Additionally, you may want separate rooms with partitions or an open floor plan.

The room may not be big enough

Furthermore, you should not assume that the sizes of those rooms on a floor plan are sufficient. There have been many instances where people have read a room's dimensions and then found that furniture does not fit correctly. Before you decide on the sizes for your house, measure everything and see where it will go. You may not be certain how much space you require. If so, measure the walking area and tables in addition to the furniture. This will help you to figure out how much room you actually need.
