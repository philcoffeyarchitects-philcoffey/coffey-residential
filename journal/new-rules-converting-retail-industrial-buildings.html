New rules for converting retail and industrial buildings

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Demand for housing is still very high in the UK. With that in mind, the announcement of reforms to the planning system will please lots of people. There will be some really big changes that could make it easier for developers to create more housing.

What is changing?

The big change is that planning requirements will be relaxing on converting vacant retail and industrial properties into homes. This is an expansion of the previous boost to the permitted development rights to make it easy to convert offices into homes.

The new broader rules will come into force in September. From then on it will be easier for developers to change old shops and other business spaces into housing. There will be no need to apply for planning permission as long as the project is creating homes. Another big change is that developers will no longer need to get planning permission in certain cases when it comes to demolishing and rebuilding. The rule will let people get rid of redundant residential and commercial buildings and replace them with new homes. Finally, there will be a fast track of planning permission when it comes to extending upwards. This means it will be easier to add new floors above current properties. However, this will be subject to consultation with neighbours.

The high street

The changes could refresh the high streets in England. It could see more old shops changing into housing and the creation of more homes above these retail spaces. The reform will also make it easier to repurpose spaces in some cases. Under the new rules there would be no need to get planning permission or local authority approval. As a result an old shop could become a cafe or office without all the red tape. However, a number of buildings will be exempt from the new rules. This includes things like libraries, pubs, and any other essential buildings. These will not have as much freedom when it comes to change of use. This is to give them more protection and reflects their importance to communities.
