Improving the home without extensions

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

For architectural projects to succeed, they must have the right people behind them. We are a firm that makes sure this is the case. This includes details like deadlines, budgets, and also strategic objectives. With homes, there are plenty of ways to enhance the feel and look without resorting to extensions. Read on to find out more.

Hallway mirrors

One thing you can do is hang mirrors in the hall. The hallway is the first thing every visitor sees when they arrive. Typically, the spaces are narrow and dark in nature. In order to optimise the room, you can install mirrors. They will also reflect light to brighten up the space.

Kitchen tiles

You should also consider looking at the kitchen tiles. You can alter this space instantly by introducing a new worktop or splash back tiles. Do a little experimenting with substitute materials like bold ceramic tiles, or coloured metals or glass. You can acquire a contemporary look this way. Merge this with a simple worktop such as wood or laminate. The former is usually the least expensive option. Depending on the material, a custom workshop is likely to range from £800 upward.

Open up the space by removing walls

It is possible to provide a building with extra space by disposing of a wall. This will make your property feel bigger and bring in additional natural daylight. Before you start though, make sure the wall is not actually a structural element. If it is load bearing you cannot remove it.
