Apartment design ideas that could work for you

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Apartments are one of the most important types of housing. They are a good option for first homes, big cities with a shortage of space, and even retirement homes in some cases. Sadly though, the design can let many of them down. In the worst cases it can make them feel claustrophobic. They can make apartments much better homes. The thing to keep in mind here is every project is unique. You need to embrace this to get the best results. Factor in things like the historical features, location, orientation, layout, and what you prefer for the design.

Balance

One of the things you need to do is make sure you get a balance with the design. Traditional and contemporary touches should work together. It is also important to ensure you are balancing the colours to make spaces warm, welcoming, and comfortable. Do the same with materials.

Views

Some apartments have an unique asset that they fail to take advantage of; the views. In London it could be the river, a park, a landmark, or even the city skyline. You should design the layout of the space to make these views the backdrop of your main living room. It will do more for the home than any artwork or decor.

Garden space

Some properties don't have the amazing views because they are on the ground floor. However, they can have their own fantastic amenity here if there is some outdoor space. Linking the interior and exterior can take the apartment to new heights. Even if it is just a courtyard and lacks traditional greenery, you can make it an useful outdoor space and connect it to your living space. If it is a traditional garden, make sure it connects and flows.

Open space

A problem with many apartments is they have walls and partitions that do more harm than good. They can make spaces dark and cramped. It can be a better option to open up the space as much as possible. If it isn't a load bearing wall, you may be able to remove or alter it. The idea should be to get rid of any dead spaces, making it so you can walk freely through as much of the apartment as possible. Let light flow too.

Check the bones

Apartments in older buildings can suffer because of alterations over the years. In some cases these additions can be hiding structural features behind plaster, paint, wallpaper, and other materials. It can be a good idea to strip back and celebrate the structural bones underneath.
