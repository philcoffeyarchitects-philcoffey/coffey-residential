Building above existing properties by using stilts

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

Creating more homes in London is tough because of lack of space. One option is to build upwards. This is why skyscrapers are so popular at the moment. It is also the reason developers are looking at adding new floors to existing buildings. Another option would be to build entirely new properties above them. Instead he commonly chose to build above buildings, rising new ones on stilts. There are several high profile structures that showcase this kind of design, including the Sharp Centre for Design at OCAD in Canada. The new apartment building will be above the five-storey Heliport House. It will rise an additional 15 storeys, creating 14 apartments and a two floor penthouse. In addition there will be a floating mirror coated pod. This will be a conference room. The current building will be redeveloped, making it feel like part of the overall design.BenefitsThis kind of project provides a number of benefits. Firstly there is less site waste because there is no demolition. Secondly the design is really eye-catching and enhances the skyline. Heliport Heights will stand out for the warm rusty appearance of the weathered steel panels and the colourful balconies. The shape is fantastic too and there will also be a roof garden. The building could leave a lasting legacy. It is likely to be the last major design by Alsop in London. It is even more impressive because the project originally got planning permission in 2014 but the developer fell into administration. Luckily finance has now been arranged. At Coffey Architects we understand why London is such a tough location for building homes. We are ready to take on the challenges and can come up with innovative solutions like the design above. We are friendly, approachable, and deliver great results.
