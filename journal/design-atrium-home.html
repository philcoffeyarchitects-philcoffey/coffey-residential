Design an atrium for your home

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

People love airy, light, spacious homes. All three things are a hallmark of modern builds, even if some of the properties are actually smaller in terms of floorspace. There are lots of different things you can do to get your dream space here. One option is to design the home around an atrium.

What are they?

When most people think about the word atrium they will imagine a large space in a public or commercial building. This could be a hotel lobby or a central stairwell. However, the idea dates back to Roman homes. Here the building would be built around an open central courtyard. Other living spaces would then connect to it. You can use the same idea today for your own home. While the size can't be on the same huge scale as a massive public building, they can still be several storeys tall. What it can create is a vast light, airy, open space within the property. You could have it above a kitchen area, a living room, or a space for hosting.

The benefits

The obvious benefit of an atrium is the light. You can design them with full glass walls and skylights to flood the space with daylight. It can create fantastic bright spaces perfect for hosting or relaxing. This can help to improve the mood of people living in the property. It also means this important part of your home isn't gloomy or uninviting. Having an atrium in your home is a real statement too. It can be the focal point of the entire property and make it stand out from every other home you will see. This is especially true if you go for nice touches like plants, statement lights, and the right decor materials. You can even change the latter to reflect the changing seasons. A great atrium design can even make your home more efficient. Firstly, you will allow far more natural light into the property. This will cut down on the need for artificial lights. You can even design the other living spaces to let light filter through and make them brighter too. Secondly, a great design can help to provide spaces with a consistent temperature by addressing heat loss and gain. It can make everything more comfortable.
