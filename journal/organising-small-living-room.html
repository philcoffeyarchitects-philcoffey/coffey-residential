Organising a small living room

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

There is a lot of variety when you look at all of the buildings in London. Our team continues to add to it with every design. This is something we have been doing for over a decade. We will have no trouble helping you with your projects, so you can count on us for everything. Organising your living room layout can sometimes feel like its own full time job. This is definitely true when yours is smaller than most. There are all kinds of questions you need to answer. In fact, we have a few ideas to share that you might like.

Length

One suggestion we have is to go long. Decorating a narrow room can be hard. Try furniture that has smaller dimensions, as well as airy, leggy pieces that lack visual weight. Put essential furnishings like big bookcases and your sofa against the wall. This will allow you to benefit from each square inch of floor space.

A multipurpose room

Don't be afraid to embrace a multipurpose design either. You might lack the space for a separate breakfast nook, living room, and dining room. If so, try carving these areas out into a single room. With furniture of the right size, such a decorating approach can make your layout appear like an open floor plan. It will have a better flow and maximise the space.

Mix it up

Finally, you can try the cosy eclectic approach. Layer a mix of objects and furnishings that are special to you. What you will end up with is a living area full of colour and texture. It can really make the space feel personal, even if it is only small.
