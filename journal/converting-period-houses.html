Converting period houses

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

A city environment might be one that makes creating unique new architecture a challenge. After all, there are already a lot of interesting buildings in most of these settings. However, our team has years of experience working in cities. Making period homes suitable and relevant for the needs of contemporary life may need a bit of thought. You have to find a way to preserve the structure's original integrity. At the same time, you must create spaces that feel modern and suit your lifestyle. If you are in need of help, we have some words of wisdom we would like to share.

Planning

Firstly, you will have to plan. This is vital if you want to avoid the risk of finding yourself at a loss. It is especially true with period properties. Stay realistic and leave a decent amount of wiggle room in terms of money and time. Keep in mind that the most successful projects start with briefs that are realistic. A decent budget does not hurt either. It should cover engineering fees, building works, and even unforeseen complications that can pop up.

Look at the history

Understanding the house's history is important as well. Discover ways to preserve the historic characteristics in the home. You can then celebrate the originality and character while adding modern touches. A good idea is to try and place yourself in the minds of the people that originally built the house. This will help you to decide on the best designs. It is often the case that the current fabric is modest and minimal. However, it is not hard to build on this sympathetically.
