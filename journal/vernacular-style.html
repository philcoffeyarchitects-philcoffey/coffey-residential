What is the vernacular style?

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

Constructing the ideal building for your project is a long process. We work to ensure that things go to plan. In order to accomplish this, we work with clients to understand their needs and design a solution. Those who take the time to travel around the UK and have taken in the sights will know one detail for sure. It is that classic vernacular structures contribute a lot to a small town or village's overall scene.

Incredible construction

You may not know what one of these houses is however. To put it simply, it is a home built using everything that was available. This is in terms of local manpower and materials. As a result it is a style that is typically synonymous with traditional, nomadic, or primitive architecture.

The building's functions

Vernacular architecture concentrates on the functions the building has to perform. From there, the design usually evolves over time. As you might expect, this architecture does come with its own benefits. In addition, you are free to design them with the local climate conditions in mind. Not to mention, they tend to perform well too. This is because they adapt to suit the purposes. Furthermore, they have a history of being energy efficient. This is due to builders using local resources. As a result the carbon footprint can be much lower.
