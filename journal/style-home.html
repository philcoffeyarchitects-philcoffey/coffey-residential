What style of home is that?

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Deciding what style of architecture a home is can be tricky. This is because there are so many different ones and no shortage of overlaps. Then, you have designs that take bits from many styles. This can make it tough to determine the styles of homes. We also have the ability to design residential architecture in any style.

Tudor

This is one of the oldest and most iconic styles in England. It is quite easy to spot because of the combination of brick on the ground floor with stucco and decorative wood trim on the second. The homes are likely to have a steep roof and can also feature prominent gables.

Georgian

This is another common style in England. The buildings are usually either brick, stone, or stucco. The most iconic features tend to be the wooden cladding. The hip roof and entrances also stand out.

Gothic revival

Some of the most amazing looking homes in England are this style. In the mid to late 19th century this was a very popular design for middle class and wealthy homes. They can be categorised by steep, complex roofs with decorative verge trim and large windows.

Italianate

Italian villa style homes can be seen in many parts of London and elsewhere in England. You can spot them because of the flat roofs, huge windows sometimes with shutters, and column porches.

Queen Anne

This is one of the most decorative housing designs. There tends to be eye catching trim and carvings. The thing to look for though is the cantilevered upper storeys, turrets and towers. Many of the properties also have wrap around porches.

Cottage style

The classic cottage style is one that lots of people in England love. The homes look quaint because they tend to be single storey with a steep roof and cross gables. Window boxes, shutters, and windows with small panes are common features.
