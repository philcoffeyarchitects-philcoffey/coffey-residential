A guide for building regulations

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

You will get excellent levels of customer service and fantastic results on your project. We support our clients by helping them from start to finish with aspects like building regulations.

What Are They?

These regulations include standards for the design and construction of building work. They apply to new builds and the alterations of existing buildings with a few exceptions. As a general rule, structural work requires approval. The regulations have a lot of detail. They also consider various aspects of the work like materials and the level of workmanship. This is along with fire safety, structural requirements, and energy conservation. Following the regulations helps ensure the quality and safety of the final work. If you fail to follow them you will not get approval. Consequently, this can result in big fines.

How Do They Differ From Planning Permission?

Building regulations look in detail at the work, including the construction and choice of materials. The purpose of planning permission is to guide how and where buildings are developed. It takes into account the appearance of the work and how it will impact the environment. This includes how people use the building and highway access.

Can You Make Building Regulation Plans Alone?

While it is possible to take on a project on your own, you should instead make sure you have the best help. This is because there are many things that you need to consider like relevant regulations for the work. You need to look at these different aspects while also creating a space that meets the need of the user. This is hard to do without the right skills and knowledge. You should be contacting a professional team of architects when you are looking for great results. We offer advice about commercial and residential architecture building projects will benefit from greatly. We have a lot of passion for our work and want to create the very best designs. Whether it is a home or an office, trust us to meet the best standards.
