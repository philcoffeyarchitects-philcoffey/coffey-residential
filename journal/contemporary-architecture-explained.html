Contemporary architecture explained

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

When a person looks for a house, they think about long term needs. We must do the same in our role as architects if we are to create properties that last. Our team shall help you to complete your project, giving you the exact support you need. Contemporary architecture is tough to define and can be very confusing. Of course, we expect this to happen. Many people think that any building built recently counts when in fact it does not. It is possible to create modern buildings with all kinds of classical designs. The thing with contemporary is that it aims to distance itself from standard practices. As a result it is innovative.

It's not a movement

What you need to understand is that this isn't an architectural movement. Buildings always relate to a distinct period. Movements include modernism, futurism, and baroque. Architectural movements are an unique choice. They claim to function as a reflection of society. Since contemporary isn't a movement, this constraint has no hold over it. It's not a school of thought. As such, this kind of design can provide you with plenty of architectural choices. Nevertheless, people can still identify certain common qualities in the architecture.

A common feature

There's one specific main feature of contemporary architecture; the introduction of new materials. This is for both exterior and interior areas. It's preferable to employ metals, wood, brick, and glass. Plants are also very common on these structures. Some choose green roofs whereas others opt for living walls. The aim with both is to make the properties more efficient and better for the environment. At Coffey Architects, we help clients in meeting all their needs. They might want to take a traditional approach or prefer something more modern. In any event, we won't stop until they're happy.
