Where should you use bi-fold and sliding doors?

Words · Simon Welch · 2 min read

The right doors can add a huge amount of value to a home. Bi-fold and sliding doors in particular can be features. Their ability to create a massive opening to connect an inner space with the wider world is amazing, especially if you have a lovely garden and views to enjoy. But, where should you use them to make the best impression?

History

Firstly though, did you know that sliding doors are much older than many people think? They actually date back thousands of years, with evidence from both Japan and Italy (including Pompeii). They were a popular choice for the elegance and space-saving.

More recently though, it wasn't really until the 80s when they began to become popular in various other parts of the world, including the UK. Farmhouse style sliding doors were common before then, but the development of lightweight aluminium bi-fold door was a major moment. It gave more options for architects. Today, you can choose high quality doors that provide amazing performance, especially in terms of functionality, energy efficiency, and rain protection.

Where to use them?

Discussing your ideas with a top architect in London is a fantastic idea, especially if you want to design a home. There's a lot to think about, especially when you can have bi-fold and sliding doors.

In the past, the most common application for bi-fold and sliding doors in homes was patios. That is still the case today, but there are many other potential applications. It's a good idea to think about where exactly you can use them.

One option that is becoming really popular is kitchens. They can be a lovely choice for open plan kitchen designs, especially when they overlook a lovely garden. Opening the doors can create a large opening and encourages dining outside with a wonderful backdrop. In turn, this can provide health benefits such as improved mood and wellbeing.

Bi-fold and sliding doors are also ideal for extensions. Whether you use it for a living space, a dining room, a playroom, or even a home office, the ability to open them wide to connect to the outside is fantastic.

It's also important to keep in mind that you don't need to limit your use of bi-fold and sliding doors to the ground floor. Have you got a balcony or roof terrace? Why not choose a door you can slide or fold to make a huge opening.
