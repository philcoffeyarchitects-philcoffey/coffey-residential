Extending and enhancing properties in conservation areas

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

We believe that architects have an inherent obligation to respect, preserve and enhance areas of historical or architectural interest. We regularly undertake public, commercial and residential projects that focus on enhancing the way people live and work whilst simultaneously preserving areas of special interest. One such project involved the design and construction of a two-storey home extension and garage to a family residence located in one of York's conservation areas. The City of York has a rich heritage. It is an unitary authority area with rural locations that extend beyond the old city boundaries. Its varied landscape and historical landmarks make it one of the most historically and architecturally significant places in the UK. The family in question have a long history of involvement in the seed trading business, and the existing property stands next to a plantation area. The focus of the project was to connect the house to the landscape with the extension and build an elevated living space and ground-level studio easily accessible by a stairway. Along with achieving this design objective, we placed a soothing and tranquil water feature outside the property to create a space for relaxation. The external space benefits from the sun that floods it with light and the surrounding leaves offer contrasting shade. The extension overall adds an impressive and contemporary touch to a 1930s housing development and perfectly represents the diverse architectural elements that can be combined to create remarkable structures. If you are considering extending an existing space or undertaking a new build, we can provide you with full assistance. From the initial stage of concept design to project management and on-site completion we are vastly experienced in all aspects of residential architecture and across the world. Our team will assemble the perfect team for your project and ensure high quality client care from start to finish.
