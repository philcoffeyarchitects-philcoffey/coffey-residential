A quick look at fire safety

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

The role of the architects London is home to incorporates various vital things. They work together to allow for successful project completion and satisfy clients. Some of the services they offer are more important than others, such as increasing the fire safety.

Crucial

Any building work needs to offer a great level of fire safety. This should minimise the risks from the smoke and heat. Along with this it needs to reduce the chance of serious or life-threatening injury to occupants as well as fire and rescue services. You should look to protect the building, contents and adjoining properties. Another consideration is to cut environmental pollution. The building should still be able to function after a fire and be repairable. Architects and designers have an influence in this through designing the fabric of the building and its contents. You should look at the combustibility or ignitability of materials. Internal finishes can also act as a fuel source so take care.

At The Heart Of The Role

An architect can help with fire safety in numerous ways. This starts out with prevention so that a fire won't start. You can achieve this through looking at potential ignition and fuel sources. After this is communication. Upon ignition, building occupants need informing and fire systems need to activate. Next you need to think about escape as people need to be able to move to somewhere safe. Containment measures help to ensure that fires do not spread and pose a serious threat to lives as well as the property. The fire then needs to be extinguished promptly without a lot of damage. Many people have seen fire safety as something that holds back innovative designs. This is far from the case due to modern advancements. Experts can create cost effective designs that meet the needs and tastes of their clients with a great standard of fire safety.
