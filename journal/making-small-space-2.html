Making the most of a small space

Words · Coffey Residential · 1 min read

Open-minded and committed, ours is a team that prides itself on its ability to find inspiration from all around them. The great work we do has encouraged various bodies, including the likes of RIBA, to praise our skill. Petite patios, tiny terraces, and small gardens are what some of us end up with at our properties. However, the fact is that they can demand more thought than bigger expanses. Regardless, you can change even the most compact plot into a dignified exterior retreat. If you're uncertain as to how you can accomplish this, then you can use the advice below to help your imagination.Treat the outside like your insideFor a moment, study your outer area as you would your inner one. We normally employ white paint to get the most out of these kinds of spots. This is due to its lightening and brightening traits. Gardens are more than capable of benefiting from this as well. With white garden furniture as well as painted brickwork, you can create a stylish space that feels bigger. If your garden is somewhat lacking in square footage, then you may want to introduce a planted terrace. This would merge low maintenance with beauty. Straightforward paving, like traditional or travertine stone, generates a homely or lustrous aesthetic. With some smart planting, you'll be able to soften everything and enhance the privacy. At Coffey Architects, we do everything we can to guarantee our clients' satisfaction. Whatever the specifics of your property or design needs, we will see to it that things go as planned. We really enjoy talking about plans and contributing our own ideas to projects. The focus is always on producing the best results at the end of the day.
