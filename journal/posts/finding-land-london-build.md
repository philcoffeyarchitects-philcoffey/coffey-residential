Consequently, we understand the dream of self builds. The access to designing and building it from scratch based on your vision is appealing. But, to achieve this in a city like London is difficult. As often the land values are high due to increasing demand and lack of supply. How to find land in LondonTo begin with, approach estate agents and attend auctions. Always tread carefully when you attend an auction though; it could result in overpaying due to competition. The good news is local authorities keep a register of available plots, specifically for self builds. This way you can search easily. Furthermore, there are some things you can keep an eye on throughout London to aid your search. In the capital, derelict buildings are often a good sign of available land. However, the city continuously demolishes and replaces structures. Therefore, look out for derelict properties that could be available. Similarly, underdeveloped properties regularly go unnoticed. Generally, the most suitable ones are a couple of storeys high, next to taller buildings. They may have potential for expansion. Also disused garages have become a common focus for development. This is mainly due to the increase in size of the modern car. So, as these garages have become redundant they are increasingly available to buy as land. Along with this, a gap between terraced houses may be suitable for development. Also known as urban infills, these plots are ideal for self builds. However, you must gain planning permission from the neighbours as well as checking no covenants remain. By sustaining a watchful eye and imaginative thought, you can find your dream plot in the city.
