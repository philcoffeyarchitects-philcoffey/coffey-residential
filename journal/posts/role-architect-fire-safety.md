As architects, whilst creating buildings, we must review both the health of the public and the fire protection present in the premises. We need to look at the property from each angle, including that of inhabitants and first responders. The architect's role with safety is imperative, and we are in fact the first individuals who must think about it. All designs must comply with the property rules that apply in our country. Additionally, we have to think proactively. It's true that building codes are the guidelines for safety. However, they typically come with major concerns to address, including layout issues. What kinds of layout issues need addressing? The flow and layout of a structure can determine how rapidly fire fighters can do their job. There should be easy access and dedicated routes to use. Some focus on staircase design, there should be distinct emphasis on landing width, numbers, and length. Architects can also look into whether elevators or stairs would work best during fire rescue scenarios.
