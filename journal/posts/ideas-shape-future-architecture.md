Over the years architectural styles and technology have changed significantly as consumer demand and trends have evolved. Now, with the amount of technology we have at our fingertips, we are anticipating some of the biggest changes ever witnessed in the industry. Here are just some of the factors that could potentially shape the future of architecture and affect the design and creation of commercial and residential architecture and across the globe.- Ageing population. Conventionally, many homes and commercial buildings have been designed with the needs of the young in mind, meaning that they aren't as suited to the older population. In recent years, it is becoming more recognised that homes need to be designed for all stages of life, with easier access and better facilities for older people.- High quality commercial experiences. As most industries are becoming more competitive and more and more businesses are showcasing their unique selling points, the demand for impressive office spaces with character has increased. Better office designs results in improved productivity, happier employees, happier clients, and an all-round improved working experience.- 4D design tools. As technology advances, we will see the introduction of a variety of new tools to help designers provide even better experiences for their clients. A majority of today's tools utilise static media and two dimensional images. It is predicted that in the next few years we will see more hybrid tools that incorporate four dimensions for an extremely accurate depiction of the finished structure. We remain at the forefront of all the latest developments in our industry and strive to produce structures which work for the people in them, whether we are creating a brand new home, a residential extension, an office building, a hotel, library, or any other commercial or private space.
