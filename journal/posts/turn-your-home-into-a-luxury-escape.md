Life tends to be stressful. It's difficult getting a break from the outside world, even when you're at home. So, you should turn yours into a real escape. Make it a retreat where you can unwind and relax. There are plenty of ways for you to elevate the property from solely a place to live to a serene haven. The following are some ideas you can use to achieve this result.

#### A spa

Firstly, you can create your own personal spa. You shouldn't settle for a standard bathroom when you can have a well-being centre in your home. Even better is the fact you won't have to deal with people in public. Choose a modern bath and/or shower that will help you to relax.

Draw upon those classic spa vibes when crafting your bathroom, using lighter shades that possess naturally calming effects. Examples include avocado green, soft champagne, and pink. Afterwards, you can add in classic spa materials like floor-to-ceiling marble or bamboo. Concrete provides a contemporary edge. Plenty of plants bring in a tropical vibe and boost the mood. They can reduce stress and lower air pollutants.

#### Hotel-like quality

Secondly, you can add hotel-level to the bedroom. We tend to sleep well on holiday because of the quality materials offered by hotels. There's no reason you can't use them at home and create bedrooms that give you the best sleep every night. You can also get advice from our team of architects in London to look at crucial things like bedroom placement and orientation.

Consider a memory foam or latex mattress instead of a classic innerspring for comfort. With sheets, 5-star hotels tend to favour Egyptian cotton. It's revered for its softness, low weight, and enabling airflow. The last touch here should be enough sumptuous pillows.

#### Install a games room

Playtime isn't solely for the children; adults also need an entertainment area too. So, release your competitive side with a games room. It can make your home more comfortable, encouraging you to take time to just enjoy yourself.

It could be something energetic like ping pong or calmer games like darts or pool. Another option would be adding a bowling alley or classic arcade games for that retro feel. Craft a mood that's conductive to escapism by having your lighting low and introducing a rug to aid with soundproofing.
