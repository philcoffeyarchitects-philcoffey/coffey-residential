The best homes support your lifestyle and maximise comfort. The most crucial thing here is striking the ideal balance between function and aesthetics. You don't need to sacrifice one for the other. In fact, you shouldn't accept compromises.

#### Purpose

The first thing you need to look at, regardless of the space you are designing, is the purpose. How will you use different spaces on a daily basis? Ultimately, you need to make sure that is the main focus. Everything should come together to facilitate the purpose.

In terms of purpose, you need to focus on the most important needs. For example, a kitchen is a place for cooking and storing food. You need to ensure both people can do it safely and efficiently. Then, you can look at other elements such as dining and hosting.

#### Materials

A key thing to look at is what materials to use. It has a direct impact on function as well as aesthetics. It's one of the most significant details, whether you're designing residential architecture, or completely different buildings.

The best place to start is looking at durable surfaces, including things like tiles, hardwood, stone, and more. They'll support the function of key spaces like kitchens and bathrooms. Just think about worktops and flooring. However, make sure the surfaces will also look, providing colour, details, and texture.

#### Storage

One of the biggest mistakes people make is they don't give enough thought to how much storage they need. As a result, rooms can easily become full of clutter. This can cause big problems, including limiting the function. It can ruin the aesthetics too.

What you need to do here is think about storage from the beginning; don't make it an afterthought that means you need to re-jig where you place furniture and other key things. There are lots of clever solutions you can use for different rooms. For example, ottoman beds mean you may not need as much additional furniture in a bedroom.

#### Lighting

A major obstacle that can have a negative impact on the function of spaces is the amount of light. A dim corner of the property won't make a very good reading room. Similarly, a dark, dank kitchen won't feel like a very nice place to cook. Make sure you think carefully and get the right kind of lighting in each space, prioritising daylight where possible. It can help you create the most incredible residential architecture.

Make sure you also think about the impact light has on the aesthetics. It can unlock a world of colour and texture. Even better, working to get more natural light into the property can provide additional aesthetic benefits such as lovely views.

#### Minimalism

As we said, clutter has a huge impact on the function of space and aesthetics. People generally prefer a minimalist approach. If you do, make sure you think carefully about the details and finishing touches. For example, if you only have a single piece of artwork on a wall, it needs to make the right impression.
