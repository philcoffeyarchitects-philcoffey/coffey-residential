One subject which we have looked at here over the years is the assumed separation of responsibility between an architect and a builder in respect of home renovation and restoration work. Most people would have a very broad, perhaps even basic, notion that it's a clear divide, with the architect responsible for the plans and the builder responsible for working from them. This is a line which has long since gone, if in fact it ever existed. An understanding of how building will progress - with a particular emphasis on the materials which are required - is a vital component of the architectural design process. The major concern of those who have renovation, extension or restoration work undertaken is that it will ultimately look like a very natural development. We believe it's fair to say that all would want changes made to their home to be unobtrusive, with the new elements perfectly blending with the existing building. In terms of achieving this it truly is vital for us, as architects in London, to be involved in every aspect of the project. As well as having an understanding of how the new work will match the existing from a practical consideration, we also need to keep informed of the requirements for materials to be used in construction. This will have a significant bearing on both our design work and the delivery of the accomplished project. We prefer to meet the above by providing you with our project management services. Our experience with all practical and administrative matters concerning building works means that you get the benefit of end-to-end knowledge at your disposal. When having changes made to an existing property you are well within your rights to expect nothing short of perfection in the work being done. We take pride in meeting - indeed exceeding - your expectation of perfection. You can view many examples of our work on our website. In several of these projects we have offered comprehensive management throughout the whole process to ensure the wishes of our clients are met.
