Not all residential properties are designed by architects; in some cases, the design is created by the property owner themselves, and in others it is done by a builder. Consulting an architect should be your first priority when you consider any form of building work, whether it is a new construction or extending an existing structure. There are many advantages to doing so, particularly in terms of the support and guidance you will receive for your residential architecture. Architects work harder than any other provider to ensure they get to know your needs and produce a suitable design. When starting out they will produce preliminary designs and develop a concept with your help. Initial sketches and models can be done to show how the property will look. Additional designs to show the landscaping around the property can also be produced. A site visit will be required to ensure all dimensions are right so the size of the plot is clear. Moving on from these early stages, the architect will be involved in refining and developing the designs where necessary. This could be done to ensure you get the kind of finish you want, or to fulfil requests from planning authorities. Sketches and models need to be turned into proper architectural drawings with all the relevant information for the builders to use. Interior designs should also be produced. With most projects related to residential architecture, the architect will need to work closely with contractors to get all of the fine details right. The contractors will have better knowledge of what can and can't be built so they can add their valuable expertise to help you avoid costly errors and reworking designs at a later date. They can also help with deciding on materials. When it comes to residential architecture it is always important to keep the needs of the inhabitants in mind. Architects are the best equipped to do this and can use their extensive knowledge to the best affect.
