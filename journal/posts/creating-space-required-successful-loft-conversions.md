In years gone by a loft conversion was considered to involve little more than putting down a bit of reinforced flooring down in an attic. The intention in these cases was seldom to use it for anything more than some extra storage space. Whilst this was a perfectly understandable approach to take it is a little outdated. In this day and age of space being at a people want to make more use of every piece of room, including in the loft. One of the aspects of our work that we have always promoted in respect of our clients' thinking is to consider the potential amount of space on offer. This is all the more true in terms of loft conversions. Whilst one might naturally think that the scope for conversion work is limited to the existing area of a loft or attic space, it's often feasible to change this. In many cases there's the margin to either lower the floor or raise the ceiling as part of the conversion project. Making this change can often have a significant impact on achieving success with a project.
