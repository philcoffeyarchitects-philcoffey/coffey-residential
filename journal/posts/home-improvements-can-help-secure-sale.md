Many experts are predicting a tough 2023 for housing in the UK. We are already seeing signs of it, particularly with people struggling to get a mortgage. There could be a drop in the number of sales next year, especially with first time buyers. This can help to increase the chance of a sale, even in the toughest times.

#### The kitchen

![Residential architecture London](images/journal/Capablanca-House-Coffey-Architects-300x264.png)

Everyone wants their home to have a beautiful kitchen. If it currently looks old, tired, and in need of an upgrade it can put people off. While you probably shouldn't invest thousands of pounds in a brand new one, there are things you can do on a smaller budget that can have a massive impact. For starters, you may want to look at the tiling to see if it needs replacing. New splashbacks can make a big difference to the kitchen design. If they look old, have lots of marks and scratches, and bring down the room, you may want to replace them. New lighting can also be a really good investment. It shouldn't break the bank and you can choose a nice looking fitting that will make the room bright and comfortable.

#### The bathroom

The second major selling point in a home, especially with residential architecture, is the bathroom. You will lose a lot of interest if it looks old and dirty. Again while a full remodel can be expensive and out of the question, there are things you can do to make it more presentable. The first thing to look at is the tiling. The last thing people want to see is grime and black marks in the grouting. You could try cleaning this to get rid of it. You may even want to think about regrouting depending on the cost. A quick new coat of paint for the walls and ceiling can also make all the difference. It can ensure the bathroom looks fresher, especially if your current paint is a few years old. Then little things like getting a new showerhead and replacing the shower curtain can really help. Some new accessories can be good too.

#### hallway

Hallways are often an afterthought, even for people who live in the property. But, when you come to sell they become very important. They are crucial for making a good first impression and people get a feel for them when they move around viewing the property. So, you need to give them some attention. The first thing to look at is the flooring. This is a high traffic area so it may need a lot of work. If you have carpet, there may be stains and soiling. It can be a good idea to give it a clean. If you have laminate or hardwood, you should check to make sure it is in good condition. On top of this you may want to think about organisation. You don't want the hallway to be messy and off-putting. Things like coat stands and shoe racks are a great idea if you have the space.

#### The living room

In terms of usage, living rooms tend to come out at the top of the list. They can be some of the most important spaces in residential architecture. So, if you are looking to secure a sale you want them to be appealing. In fact, you will most likely be using it as the staging room for viewings. With that in mind you need it to make the right impression. One thing you can do here that won't cost you anything at all is declutter. That is important before you take pictures to sell the house and during viewings. You want people to see the potential of the space and imagine their things here rather than just seeing a busy, overcrowded room. A new coat of paint can be a good idea too. It is likely you have the decor how you want it. However, that may not appeal to potential buyers. It can be a better idea to paint a neutral colour. It can make the space feel cleaner, newer, and brighter.
