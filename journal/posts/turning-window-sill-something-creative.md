London's incredible variety in relation to architecture is part of its charm. It provides the entire city with a background that celebrates innovation and creativity. If there is one thing you should try with your home, it is getting creative with your window sill designs. Doing so can add interest and character to the property. The sills serve a practical purpose, as they bridge the gap between window frame and wall. However, with the right design they can carry out other jobs, like storage and seating. What we are going to do is go over some of the things you can do with yours to get more out of them.

#### Seating

![Residential architecture London](images/journal/shutterstock_225532654-300x200.jpg)

Firstly, you can make a window sill seat. Plenty of seating ideas exist that look wonderful. At the same time, they will add a degree of character and warmth to any sort of interior scheme. What's more, they can make the most of the space you have available. Within a bedroom, a window seat will be an excellent idea. You can create one instead of trying to force a sofa or chair inside. It is a much better use of the space and can become a favourite spot for reading and relaxing. In truth, any room can benefit from a window seat. People will be encouraged to take time out to just enjoy the views.

#### A statement circular window

Something else you can try is introducing a statement circular window with a decorative sill or seating. Round windows add a considerable amount of architectural merit to homes of any style. They supply interest and character, as well as a real degree of individuality.

#### Breakfast bars

The third suggestion we have is to create a breakfast bar with a view. You may be after small kitchen ideas. If so, this one is ideal for those kitchens where there is a lack of space for islands or typical breakfast bars. Instead you can build your window sill out to enable a high chair or stool to fit beneath. Also, think about using materials that match your other kitchen worktops.

#### Bath surrounds

Finally, you should know that window sills can double as a bath surround. This is a clever approach if you need to address the requirement for more bathroom storage space. You can use a particularly deep sill to hide your pull-out shower and tap pipework. At the same time, you will be able to offer a practical bath surround.
