Every home needs windows to ensure interiors are comfortable. They get light and air into the property, ensuring it is habitable. In addition, they can frame beautiful views and provide connectivity to the wider world. Choosing the right type can be tricky though because there are so many options. Luckily, [we can help](https://www.coffeyarchitects.com/projects), especially with residential architecture.

### Three factors

The ideal windows will provide the perfect balance of durability and integrity, functionality, and aesthetics. They'll make everyday life more pleasant for inhabitants of the property.

#### Structural properties

![Residential architecture London](images/journal/Cove-Cottage-view-300x189.png)

Firstly, every window must be stable and durable. The last thing you want is a strong gust of wind to blow it right out of the frame. Similarly, you don't want to risk the glass cracking or shattering. There are a number of factors to think about here. Does the glass have sufficient structural properties? It is more important if you choose modern minimalist designs. Here the glass itself provides much of the support, augmented by the frame. With larger windows, the glazing must be more self supporting and stable. In addition, you have to think about the weather. Good windows need effective wind resistance and have to remain watertight. They also need to withstand UV rays and both high and low temperatures. If you look at residential architecture specifically, windows need a few more vital properties. It can be very noisy in different parts of the city. Therefore, the windows need to deliver sufficient sound insulation too.

#### Function

In many cases the core function of windows is to open and close to allow ventilation. They also allow light into the property. However, it is more complex than these factors alone. Ultimately, they need to ensure spaces are comfortable at any time. So, they have to provide the right balance of light. Another function of the windows is to enhance the space. One way to do this is by framing beautiful views. A second is to make spaces feel bigger by connecting to outside. What you need to think about here is how the windows will open. Are classic options like swing or sliding sufficient? Or would a more modern take like recessing into the wall or ceiling be better?

#### Aesthetics

In fact, they are one of the most crucial decorative elements on homes. They need to work with the architectural design too. One thing to look at here is how to provide visual continuity. Lots of people want windows today that are almost seamless connections between the interior and exterior. That is why things like thin aluminium frames are really popular. They can achieve a better transition. Having the frame colours match the interior works too, making the frame almost disappear.
