As the years have gone by, we've had the opportunity to work on a large variety of buildings. As a result our team has been able to develop their skill sets to the point where they are considered to be incredibly flexible. If the subject of rainscreen cladding ever comes up, chances are that you will form a mental picture in your head of a commercial structure like an office or warehouse. However, a fact that you may not be aware of is that residential constructs can also take advantage of this cladding. The main reason why is that the same technical and aesthetic principles that apply with commercial buildings exist with homes too. It's becoming more apparent to professionals like us that rainscreen cladding can provide residential structures with a more dynamic appearance at a reduced price. The panel's lightweight characteristics, in addition to the simple mounting, means not much weight is added to properties. This is of far greater convenience with every extra storey. Furthermore, it's none too difficult to factor colour into a plan. Something else to note about rainscreen cladding is that it performs brilliantly in areas prone to high moisture levels. Whenever there are strong winds afoot, a certain degree of velocity is added onto the rain as it makes contact with a building. The secondary layer supplied by the cladding is able to diminish the potency of such elements, whilst augmenting the premises' acoustic capabilities at the same time. Whether you are simply looking to extend an existing property, or produce an entirely new build, we shall discuss every stage of the process with you, and offer input if we feel that something would work better if done differently.
