Homes can be very different if you look at urban and rural areas. Crucial differences can include the size, layout, fittings, and even the orientation. While it may seem strange, some people want to build an urban style property in a rural spot. We want to look at that here to see why and how to do it.

#### Designs, fixtures and fittings

Housing in cities tends to be smaller because of the lack of space. However, these properties can teach people a number of important lessons. While they can be compact, they can be more adaptable. A single room might need to be able to change it up to be an office one moment and a playroom or gym the next. This is a great idea to keep in mind for any home. Urban homes also tend to have more contemporary fixtures and fittings. They are usually quicker to adapt to changes in tech, especially those that improve quality of life and convenience. Plus, you can often find that housing in cities is more energy efficient.

#### What to think about?

![Residential architecture London](images/journal/Rural-300x199.jpg)

If you are looking at creating an urban home in a rural area, there are a number of things to keep in mind. They will help you create better homes, whether you are thinking about residential architecture. Firstly, you need to think about the location. Generally you can't just start building in the middle of a field. That would damage the landscape. It would also cause a number of potential issues if you weren't near any amenities. You need to choose the spot carefully. Crucially, ensure you will have space and great views. They are the two biggest benefits of rural living. Secondly, you need to think about materials. Popular choices for urban areas like steel and concrete might not look very nice in countryside settings. Instead, you will probably want to go with something more rustic. Ensure they fit with the area so it doesn't cause damage. Modern and contemporary design touches are the best option here. One thing in particular to go with is massive windows. They will ensure the property makes the most of the rural backdrop. Why bother with walls and decorating if you have country views right outside; you should use those to frame the rooms instead.

#### Benefits

There are plenty of fantastic advantages if you do this kind of project. It could ensure you create some of the best residential architecture or whatever area you are targeting. For one thing, generally it is more cost effective to choose an urban style home. You can often save on construction costs even when you have a design. You can also tackle space restrictions. Generally rural areas are less dense and there is more space on plots. What this means is you can choose an urban design with everything you want. Maybe a full length pool isn't possible in a big city, but it could be in a rural spot. Additional outdoor space in rural areas can be wonderful too. It means you could have even more amenities outside.
