If you want your architecture to stand out, you need to work with people that know the industry and have the creativity to push boundaries. For years, we been focusing on using techniques and designs that others may not normally consider. The result is properties unlike any their owners have seen before. There is something so attractive about stepping into a house that feels like it is your home. This is especially true when it mirrors your personality brilliantly. You could have an interior that presents nicely. It might possess a practical layout, first rate finishes, and furniture. However, that homely vibe might not be there. If you do some tweaking and add new things though, you should be able to transform your house into a home.

#### Floor finishes

One way you can do this is by concentrating on your floors. If you leave a floor barren it is going to highlight the emptiness because nothing can absorb the sound, producing echoes. This can happen whether it is bare concrete, boards, or even tiles. By layering in textiles, it shall make the space more homely straight away. Think about laying a rug down underfoot to warm the interior up. Use the flooring to define your space and add more personality.

#### Use the wall space

Make great use of your walls too. Some of you may be short on floor space. If you are, you can use your walls to add more interest. Additionally, this enhances the interior's functionality. Use simple shelving with L-brackets and neatly filled up with sentimental objects. This can produce an excellent visual impact. Don't be afraid to choose high shelves either; there is often plenty of space to use here that could save you having things on the floor.
