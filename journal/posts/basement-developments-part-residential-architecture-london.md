Let's not hide away from the truth - basement developments are amongst the most controversial subjects you can raise in respect of residential architecture. They split opinion, with some in favour and others against. Fortunately we always look at this kind of project from the informed position we have earned thanks to our own architectural work. When plans feature on the news, they tend to be ones of a large and ambitious scope, normally intended for the homes of the rich and famous. It would be a mistake, however, to think that this is the case for each and every basement design. The term is broad, and architectural work such as a drop in the floor level to create a higher ceiling or the creation of a fairly rudimentary cellar would also constitute as a this kind of development. With respect to the latter, we believe there is value in emphasising the fact that basement developments are not a new phenomenon in London. A defining aspect of Victorian architecture was the inclusion of a cellar in new property designs. Interestingly, the size of these cellars was just as controversial a matter then as the size of basement developments now tend to become. From our architectural experience and perspective we would always seek to cite the many benefits afforded through basement developments in London. We know the subject is not without controversy, but this should not imply that all such work is by default controversial. As long as the correct process is followed, which is to say comprehensive plans being drawn up and approval being obtained from the relevant authorities, then there should not be an issue with this change being made to a property.
