2014 has been an interesting year for residential and commercial architecture, and has seen many designers look beyond the structure itself to the surrounding environment. More and more properties are being designed to contribute to their landscapes, offering enhancements rather than taking something away from them. As part of this there has been a shift in how people look at landscaping with designers taking the chance to make the best use of open spaces around properties. Where gardens were once peripheral, they are now essential parts of the design and bring the internal and external environments together. It can now be said that landscape architecture and property design are on equal footing and both need to be considered together to produce the best results. In cities around the world there are beautiful landscaping projects taking place that enrich the environment. It is important to keep greenery in mind with projects, particularly when you consider the benefits it offers in terms of keeping the air clean and boosting relaxation. Plants naturally remove contaminants from the air and can also provide soundproofing, making them a fantastic addition to most exteriors. With landscape architecture you can design the environment around the property and ensure they complement each other. Every project should take into account the impact on the surrounding area, even when it is something basic like a simple extension or a new conservatory. With larger projects there should be even more emphasis on protecting the landscape and utilising it to enhance the site. The best examples of landscape architecture include things like healing and contemplation gardens. Designed spaces like these can be immensely beautiful whilst simultaneously improving conditions by reducing soil erosion, drainage and the amount of contaminants in the air. The settings can help properties become more sustainable and also enrich the surrounding area. When thinking about residential or commercial architecture it is important to keep the landscaping in mind. You should make sure you are utilising the space to the best effect, creating a space that is functional, relaxing and offering all of the benefits you want from it.
