Housing comes in all shapes and sizes. Look at any city in the UK and you can get a good idea of this. You may see everything from blocks of apartments to large family homes or even narrow houses. If you look closer, you can also see private homes as well as social housing. At Coffey Architects, we have the skills to [create any kind of home](coffeyarchitects.com/project/). Sadly, the UK lacks enough homes for low-income earners. Regardless of the increasing demand, we have not seen a big increase in social housing for a long time. Funding for such projects has also been cut frequently. This is a really awful situation and one that needs addressing. In this post, we will look at some of the reasons why we need more of this type of housing.

#### Mental health and wellbeing

Housing plays an essential role in a person's mental health and wellbeing. According to research, people who have problems finding a suitable home are at a greater risk of developing stress. There are also more studies that reveal a lot of people with mental health issues are residing in rented housing. This can occur because they feel they will never be able to buy their own home and don't have stability. By offering more affordable, safe, and quality housing, we can improve health and wellbeing considerably. Those who live in social housing will be happy that they have a stable home, free of the worry that comes with pricey mortgages or rent.

#### Thinking of the future

Social housing will also be able to benefit future generations. The majority of families living in these homes have kids. There is a link between the quality of the child's home and their wellbeing. So, providing more safe, good quality homes will have a positive influence that will follow them for the remainder of their lives. Investing in this kind of housing also means the councils have assets they can use for many years. Local authorities can manage the properties carefully to maintain the quality for a very long time.
