Whenever someone thinks of the boundary of a small city garden or the walls of a room, something straight and solid comes to mind. Straight walls do provide clean, crisp lines. However, curved walls give you something entirely different. It is true they are not as common. Yet, walls that automatically bend and curve offer a sense of energy and flow to a space.

#### Building curves into the architecture

At times, you build the curves into the structure's architecture. As a result, you need to incorporate them into the room's scheme in a clever way. In many cases though, you can add a curved wall to function as a foil to robust horizontal and vertical lines. In addition, they can be a zoning device or partition. They divide the space softly without appearing obtrusive. With curling, smooth lines, it is possible to boost that decent feeling and enhance functionality outside and inside. In some instances, it is the building's architecture that curves. If you reside in a property with soft lines as opposed to straight ones, you should work with them. Bold and long horizontal lines drag attention towards any curving walls. As a result they will end up leading your eyes around the space.
