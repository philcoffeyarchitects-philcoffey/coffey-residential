For all 21st century homes, the norm should be to construct with sustainability in mind. What we plan to do here is present you with a series of tips on sustainable housing. What tips can I use? Not everyone shall choose this approach, but the fact is that smaller houses are more productive. Reducing the size will lower your energy needs and material usage. Due to the heavier requirements for cooling and heating, larger homes utilise more resources. Consider the essentials and don't overemphasise once it comes to deciding on size. You may want to think about employing local materials as well. This will significantly reduce the need for shipping. Additionally, it's greener to purchase locally milled wood as opposed to having some sent from somewhere miles away. Just remember that the availability of these mediums shall depend on where you wish to construct the house. Something else you'll require is efficient doors and windows. To keep the elements at bay, they should be equipped with ample weather stripping. They must also close tight enough that the opening becomes sealed. This reduces how much heat escapes and lowers your energy consumption. At Coffey Architects, we can advise you on every aspect of building a sustainable home.
