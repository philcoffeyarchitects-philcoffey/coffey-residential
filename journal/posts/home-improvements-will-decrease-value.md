You may be a homeowner that is under the assumption that renovating your home will boost its value instantly. In reality, renovations, like everything else, aren't created equally. Generally speaking, it is a safe bet to upgrade your kitchen. Should you overdo it however, there is a chance you won't recoup your investment. Plus, if you enhance one room while the others remain old and out of date, buyers will only focus on the areas that need improvement. There are also multiple renovations that will lower the value of your home. For anyone looking to sell, you will want to be careful about the improvements you decide to make.

### Some general advice

Before we talk specifics, there are a few points you must keep in mind with remodelling. One of the most important is to reign in your personal tastes. It could prove tricky for prospective buyers to imagine themselves in a leopard-print bedroom. Many individuals also won't like the idea of repairing or stripping wallpaper in every room. Stick to neutrality to let your home appeal to more people. Ensure that you keep all your enhancements in the same flavour and style of the property as a whole. Having one perfect room and ignoring the others is never going to work. A nice balance is necessary so prospective buyers don’t get underwhelmed or overwhelmed.

#### Some specific things to avoid

Now let's look at projects to stay away from unless you intend to stay in your home permanently. Things like these few examples below can lower the value. Firstly, don't install a pool if you don't have the climate for it. If it is too cold, buyers will only see it as a liability. Jacuzzis and hot tubs can be a bad idea too. Not everyone likes them and they require upkeep. You might be considering converting your garage into a playroom or home office. It may be a good idea for you, but other buyers may prefer having somewhere to store their car and other items. So, it could be an investment to avoid. Like we said before as well, don't do additions if they won't fit with the home's overall style. With all this knowledge in hand, you should be able to dodge making home improvements that will lower the value. Just remember to focus on what will appeal to potential buyers and what could deter them.
