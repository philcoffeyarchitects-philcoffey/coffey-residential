It can be a challenge to build new architecture, especially if it is your intention to be unique. Having been in the industry for as long as we have however, our team is more than capable of helping you. We demand a lot from the kitchen and its individual components. It is at the centre of a lot of the action on a daily basis. We unpack our shopping here, prepare meals, and experience spills. As a result, it is critical that you use the correct materials for the job. To help you, we are going to discuss some kitchen worktop ideas in this post.

#### Will classic touches work?

![Residential architecture London](images/journal/Urban-Loft-Coffey-Architects-300x203.png)

One of the first things you can try is rethinking classic styles. When trends change, so should your way of thinking about what is right for the aesthetics. For example, wood is not simply for country or traditional kitchens anymore. Thanks to new contemporary finishes, you have far more freedom to use it where you like. Don't hesitate to rip up the rule book here. You can use worktops in your design to produce an interesting contrast between trends and styles by selecting the unexpected. It can make surfaces a real high point.

#### Unite surfaces

You can also try uniting separate surfaces by using the same worktop. Generate a cohesive look by working with the same material across each surface. This could be counters, islands and breakfast bars. It will work really well in a smaller kitchen because it keeps the appearance concise. However, it also works with bigger kitchens to stop the separate areas from feeling adrift from each other. You can also go one step further here. By matching your worktop colour to the kitchen cabinets, you will help them feel more integrated as a look.
