We're almost half way into the first month of 2026, so most people will be making progress on their New Year resolutions. It's the busiest time of the year for positive changes, including eating healthier, getting more active, and starting new hobbies. Not surprisingly, it is also a busy time for people thinking about home renovations. If you are, we want to remind you how important it is to focus on the intent here.

### The main focus

It's easy to go off on a tangent with home renovations, especially when you start looking at trends and all the different advice out there. If you aren't careful, you can move too far away from the original intention of the project. Make sure you keep attention on your main goals; making your home more comfortable for day to day life. It can help you to avoid mistakes or missteps, some of which can be costly to rectify in the future.

The thing here is to focus on the key foundations of good designs. If you get these elements right you can improve your home considerably. Even better, you can ensure you get a better ROI for your renovation. The last thing you want to do is invest only to find you need to put more money into undoing it.

#### Lighting

The one element that is important in every single project is the light. It affects everything, including mood, energy levels, and much more. So, ensure you make the right decisions when it comes to orientation, light diffusion, and brightness. Think about opportunities to get more natural light into rooms, including things like skylights or floor to ceiling windows.

#### Proportions

Did you know that the UK has some of the smallest homes in Europe? Luckily, we develop all kinds of innovative ways to do more with less. If you are renovating, it can be tempting to go big with spaces. However, you risk making the home feel awkward and uncomfortable, especially if you get it wrong with the size of key things like furniture and kitchen islands. Make sure you always focus on proportions and the ultimate intent to ensure comfort and wellbeing.

#### The flow

What is one of the most jarring things about architecture? Awkward transitions between spaces. Ideally, you should be able to walk through a home with ease. There should be a sense of harmony, ensuring spaces connect seamlessly even if they are for wildly different uses. Make sure you consider this, particularly when deciding where to have walls.

#### Materials

Finally, it is essential that you think carefully about what materials you are using. Remember the intent here. The right selections can make a home comfortable and improve wellbeing. On the other hand, the wrong materials can cause problems. The best thing to do is invest in quality products that will last, especially for key things like kitchen counters. Plus, look back at the lighting and consider how it will impact the materials. Good lighting can showcase the texture and fine details that people love, making spaces more interesting.
