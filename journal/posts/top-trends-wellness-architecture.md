One of the great things about architecture is how easily wellness and sustainability can work together. You can design a building that will improve the quality of life of the inhabitants and also make it gentle on the environment. There are lots of things you can do, from saving energy and water to ensuring good air quality. We will look at three of the top trends in wellness architecture below. As a result you can get better ideas for your project.

#### Multi-use rooms

Modern lifestyles shape what people expect from rooms. Now you can see more focus on experiences and social interaction. This is notable in kitchens. Now spaces are larger and tend to be open plan so that people cooking can easily speak with family members and friends. In addition there is more use of technology to make rooms serve different purposes.

#### Awareness of building materials

In the past people did not pay too much attention to the building materials. Now there is far more awareness of sustainability and how different things can affect air quality. While choosing eco-friendly substances is still vital, lots of people go one step further and choose ones that are healthy, ensuring they are toxic free.

#### Plants and greenery

More and more people are learning about the benefits of incorporating greenery into buildings. This could be as simple as having plants inside. On the other hand green tech can be an actual part of the building, whether it is vertical or roof gardens. Some people are even using plants to detect things like air quality and daylight. People are drawn to living features because they understand the array of benefits they offer. These three trends are inspiring people to create better buildings.
