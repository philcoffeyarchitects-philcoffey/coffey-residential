More frequent extreme weather, including hotter summers, colder winters, and windy periods, puts a lot of pressure on homes. It can lead to lots of problems, including big repair bills and an impact on quality of life. #### The roof

A good quality roof is one of the most important barriers against the weather. It needs to keep the elements out, while also effectively resisting them so you don't need to worry about frequent repairs. On top of that, it needs to offer good ventilation to allow circulation and prevent issues like mould. Finally, you need sufficient gutters and downspouts to manage water.

There is a lot to think about if you are looking at roof renovations to make a home more weather resistant. What kind of design do you need? Does it need a steep pitch to efficiently get rid of a high volume of water? Will it have enough wind resistance? How durable are the materials you're planning to use? Will they be able to resist high and low temperatures as well as rain, ice, and snow?

#### Windows and doors

A great roof and sturdy walls are crucial for weather resistance. However, you must avoid compromising their quality by choosing weak windows and doors. They are typically the most vulnerable parts of homes because they are physical openings in the envelope. If you are renovating, it's a very good idea to look at them and consider upgrades if they aren't up to standard.

Ideally you should select sturdy windows and doors made from durable materials. Double or triple glazing is a safe choice. Weather stripping is a great investment too because it can further prevent water ingress, draughts, and other issues. All of this can help to improve energy efficiency too.

#### Insulation

One of the reasons many homes in the UK struggle is they lack quality insulation. It means properties can overheat in summer or become uncomfortably cold in the winter. Investing in better insulation, or using construction materials with insulating properties, can help make homes more weather resistant.

#### Landscaping

It helps to look at the space around your home as well as the structure itself. The landscaping can have a huge impact on weather resistance. For example, if you've got lots of impermeable materials it can actually make a home warmer and increases the risk of flooding. This is important to think about when you're creating residential architecture, especially built up locations where materials like this are very common.

Renovating gardens, driveways, and other external spaces can do a lot of good. Most importantly, make sure you have sufficient grading to keep water from your foundations. Using permeable materials to prevent water from pooling is also wise. Rain gardens are a great idea to manage rainfall. Trees, shrubs, and other greenery can provide cooling in hot months too.
