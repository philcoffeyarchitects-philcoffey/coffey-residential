In architecture there is a constant search to balance internal and external designs to create properties that satisfy all stakeholders. It is a challenge on every project due to the impact decisions for one can have on the other. The World Interior of the Year awards celebrate all of the great designs created inside properties. Work on the 2017 edition of the event is currently ongoing, including the narrowing down of the number of projects in the running for awards. In excess of 200 properties from across the globe were on the original longlist. These were recently cut to 78 after careful judging. All of the 78 projects are and have pushed the boundaries of what can be done with interior design. There are beautiful retail spaces, offices, residential properties, hotels, and even culture, civic, and transport designs. An impressive 8 of the projects are based in the UK. The property is based close to Buckingham Palace and houses 170 apartments. Each home has a wonderful internal layout, features materials, and has been finished to an excellent standard. One of the most incredible projects on the shortlist is the new Grand Entrance Hall of Harrods. The most famous department store in the world is now home to a stylish entrance that captures the heritage of the Grade II listed building whilst also introducing a modern twist. Original features are preserved and sit comfortably beside modern nickel-bronze escalators and a glass dome roof. A project that is unashamedly modern is Google's UK office in Kings Cross. The stand out feature of the interior is that meeting rooms can be adapted whenever necessary to suit different needs. In addition there is a full 'mini city' inside the building with all kinds of facilities for employees to use, including restaurants, training rooms, a gym, and relaxation spaces. The Inside World Festival of Interiors is being held in Berlin between the 15th and 17th of November. The winners of the best interiors of the year will be judged during it.
