You can find many different types of home in urban areas. However, the most common type tends to be apartments to maximise the amount of housing for the available land. Good design is important here to get the most from the space. However, you should not underestimate the power of colour. It can enhance every area, particularly kitchens.

#### Why is it important?

Urban apartments can feel small and cramped, especially in poorly designed buildings. It can lead to all kinds of negative feelings for the residents. However, colour can change everything. It has the power to make spaces feel bigger, calmer, and cosier. The right colours can enhance awareness and alertness too. Kitchens in apartments are great places to use colour effectively. Many designs have them tucked away. Even if they are part of a multifunctional space, generally they focus on practicality rather than having design elements that stand out. This is missing a great opportunity to make the kitchen a focal point of the home. The right colours could make them one of the most vibrant and productive spaces.

#### What to do?

![Residential architecture London](images/journal/Dieting-300x196.jpg)

The best thing about using colours in kitchens, even small ones in apartments, is you can celebrate your personality. Bright colours can be a great touch if you are outgoing and energetic. Use them on things like cabinets, tiles, a feature wall, or statement pieces. It can make residential architecture and other areas much better. If you want colour but don't want it to be too bright and vibrant, softer pastel shades could be exactly what you want. The great thing here is the softness of the colours can help make spaces feel more calming. This can be useful for kitchens where too much stress can lead to accidents. Natural pastels like mint green are a great choice. Neutral colours can work very well too, similarly to what you get with pastel. They look effortless and stylish. A really good idea is to use them to highlight architectural features. Another good idea is to think about contrast. You can make a really bold statement by contrasting a dark colour or a vibrant one with lighter shades. It can give a sense of excitement and energy. One thing many people dislike about urban settings and apartments is the lack of greenery. However, you can choose colour for kitchens to ensure they aren't drab and colourless. You can also easily grow various things in many kitchens, from simple herbs to flowers. Keep in mind they improve air quality too.
