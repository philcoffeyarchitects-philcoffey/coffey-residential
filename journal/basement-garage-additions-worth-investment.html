Basement and garage additions are worth the investment

Words · Coffey Residential · 2 min read

Everyone wants to live in a place they can call their own. The trouble is that there are countless properties out there that look too similar to one another. We can aid you in producing something that is truly distinct.

You might be someone that is outgrowing your home. Before making the decision to move, you should consider the options for adding to the property. These are normally wonderful investments since they increase the square footage as well as the building's value. With more space you may be able to save yourself from a move. People get home additions for all sorts of reasons. For instance, there could be ageing relatives that need an in-law suite. Having more kids can also increase the need for bathrooms and bedrooms. If you are looking at the options, we have some ideas to share.

Basements

Firstly, we want to talk about basement home additions. If you already have one, you are halfway there. You could invest in converting it into new living space. This can be a more cost effective option than building an above-ground extension. If you don't have a basement already, you could look at adding one. It can be a big investment and bring lots of upheaval, but the rewards can be fantastic. A good project can increase your living space without having to lose any outdoor room at ground level.

Garages

You may own a garage that lacks a second storey. What you can do here is add one to produce the extra space you need for your family. Prior to starting the work, it is a good idea to check that the garage's structure and foundation can support an extra floor. It may even be possible to produce a studio apartment with a kitchenette and bathroom if you have the room.
